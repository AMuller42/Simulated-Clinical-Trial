A Phase III Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Trial Evaluating the Efficacy of a Novel Oral Therapy for Knee Osteoarthritis

Disease: Knee osteoarthritis
Intervention: Once-daily oral Drug X
Control: Matching placebo
Patients: 300 adults aged 45–80 with moderate knee osteoarthritis
Follow-up: 24 weeks, with assessments at baseline, 4, 12, and 24 weeks
Primary Outcome: Change in a validated pain score from baseline to week 24
Secondary Outcomes: Physical function score, walking distance, quality of life, incidence of adverse events, and treatment discontinuation
Statistical Features to Simulate: Randomization, baseline covariate imbalance by chance, patient adherence, dropout, missing outcome data, adverse events, and varying treatment effect sizes

Steps
1. Generate Patients
2. Randomize to treatment or Control
3. Generate Outcomes
4. Analyze results
5. Repeat Trial
6. Ask more questions and consider things like
    Does increasing the sample size improve power?
    What happens if dropout doubles?
    What if the treatment effect is smaller than expected?
    How does variance affect statistical significance?
    What happens if baseline characteristics are imbalanced?
